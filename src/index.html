The venue

We are getting married in the Russell Suite of Pembroke Lodge, Richmond Park, Queen Street, Richmond TW10 5HX.

You'll need to enter and exit the park via Richmond Gate and then head up the right-hand fork at the roundabout, and you'll reach a large public car park. The Lodge is directly opposite. Head towards the main entrance of the lodge and there’ll be someone there to direct you to the right place.

Please note that the Russell Suite is on the first floor of the building, with access only via a staircase.

On the day

The dress code is smart, but not overly formal - we’d like suits and dresses, but hats aren’t necessary and colourful patterned socks are actively encouraged.

12:30pm

The wedding ceremony starts at 1pm, so we recommend that you arrive from 12:30pm.

3pm

After the ceremony there will be drinks and canapes while photos are being taken, with the meal beginning at around 3pm. Because it’s mid-afternoon, we would recommend that you've had a hearty breakfast or light early lunch before coming to the ceremony.

7pm

After the meal there will be reception drinks, with the DJ and dancing up and running from 7pm. There will be a buffet in the evening so you've got loads of energy for dancing..

11pm

We get the park to ourselves after dusk, but because of restrictions the party will finish at 11pm.

Local area

There’s a list of Richmond hotels on Tripadvisor at this link. We’re staying at the Richmond Hill Hotel, but a lot of the local hotels (unsurprisingly) are expensive so you may prefer to look further afield.

The last District line train leaves Richmond at 00:03, so you'll have plenty of time to get on the Tube at the end of the night.

We’ve been to the Marlborough and Jackson & Rye before and would recommend both for food, if you're looking for that pre-wedding breakfast. Plus, the Ole & Steen bakery on the High Street is one of our favourite places if you're looking to grab a quick coffee pastry, sausage roll or sandwich.

Just a warning - Richmond Station and Richmond High Street are at the bottom of a large hill, and our venue is at the top. It probably doesn’t look like a long walk on Google Maps, but please bear the hill in mind. Unfortunately the buses don’t make it all the way up the hill, so an Uber or taxi is probably your best bet for getting up to the venue in a relaxed way.
